Biography

I am a PhD candidate with Artificial Intelligence for Energy group (AI4E), IDLab, Ghent university, Belgium, where I am working with this amazing team. My research focus is on physics-based machine learning, reinforcement learning, and statistical modeling for designing efficient control for different demand response applications. I explored soft robotic applications of physics-based machine leaning models as differenciable simulators during my research visit at Robust autonomy and decisions lab, University of Edinburgh. Currently, I am also exploring machine learning to develop hydrological earth twins as a machine learning researcher for Frontier Development Labs.

I spend my evenings listening to good music, or practicing a musical instrument (currently learning Bansuri). Reading a good book, or writing one myself. I have also invested time of my life on travelling and reading graphic novels.
